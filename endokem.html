Endocrinology

The Paediatric Endocrinology Unit at KEM Hospital, Pune is a comprehensive facility for the diagnosis and treatment of all hormonal disorders occurring in children from birth until 18 years of age.
Hormonal disorders involve the hormone secreting (endocrine) glands in the body and usually involve an excess or deficiency of hormones or their action caused by a genetic / developmental or acquired disorder. These may be manifest from birth to adolescence.

Hormonal disorders involve the hormone secreting (endocrine) glands in the body and usually involve an excess or deficiency of hormones or their action caused by a genetic / developmental or acquired disorder. These may be manifest from birth to adolescence.

  • Growth disorders, such as short stature and obesity
  • Thyroid disorders
  • Childhood diabetes, including insulin and non-insulin dependent
  • Puberty disorders, such as delayed and precious (occurring too early) puberty
  • Menstrual disorders in adolescents including PCOD (Polycystic Ovarian Disorder)
  • Bone and calcium metabolism disorders, including those related to Vitamin D deficiency or resistance, such as rickets, disorders of bone fragility causing fractures, and others
  • Adrenal glands disorders, including excess steroid drug us
  • Pituitary gland disorders
  • Hormonal dysfunctions occurring in children with Beta Thalassemia Major as a consequence of receiving frequent blood transfusions
  • Endocrine (hormonal) consequences in survivors of childhood cancer
  • Disorders manifesting with atypical external genitalia (disorders of sexual differentiation) in newborns and their consequences and management in childhood and adolescence
  • Hormonal disorders causing low blood sugars (hypoglycemic disorders) from neonatal period upto adolescence
