Neonatology

    The Department of Neonatology at the Lokmanya Tilak Municipal medical College & General Hospital runs a state -of art 40 bedded Neonatal Intensive care Unit that provides yeoman services to sick and preterm newborn babies born in the hospital as well as those referred from outside for specialized care.


    To deal with the increasing influx of patients, urology OPD was conducted twice weekly. The first kidney transplant was performed in Bombay in 1977 and subsequently a regular transplant programme was established at LTMG Hospital. The department of urology has been carrying out live related kidney transplantation regularly. On 27th March 1997, the first cadaver kidney transplantation in the state of Maharashtra was carried out by us. LTMGH is designated as the Zonal Co-Ordination Centre for Cadaver Transplant in Maharashtra. We have a separate transplant OPD on every Saturday.A separate Andrology OPD was established in 1992.


    The Department was established in 1992 by Dr. Armida Fernandez, then Professor & Head of the Department, Dr. JayashreeMondkar, then Associate Professor & Dr. Rajesh Savargaonkar , Lecturer in the Department..


    The department has to its credit the establishment of the first Human Milk Bank In Asia, established on 27th November 1989 and is currently in the 27th year of its existence.

    Courses Offered:

    DM Neonatology Course

    The Department is recognized by the Medical Council of India for the Post-Doctoral course of DM in Neonatology.

    One year fellowship in Neonatology from the Maharashtra University of Health Sciences 2 seats per year.

    Besides offering quality care to these tiny babies, the unit also serves to train undergraduate and post graduate students, nurses and observers from across the country in the art of neonatal care.

About us

Lokmanya Tilak Municipal Medical College and General Hospital is a full-fledged Government medical college located in Mumbai, Maharashtra. The college imparts the degree Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS). It is one of the most prestigious medical colleges in India. It is recognised by the Medical Council of India. This is one of the oldest medical colleges in Mumbai. Lokmanya Tilak municipal medical college hospital is also locally known as Sion Hospital
